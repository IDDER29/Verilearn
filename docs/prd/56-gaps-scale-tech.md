## Critique & Gaps — Scalability, Reliability & Technical Constraints

**The core tension this lens exposes:** VeriLearn's entire promise — *nothing unverified ships; every claim is checked; trust is always live and never stale* — is a set of **hard correctness invariants running on top of the slowest, most expensive, least-reliable primitives in the stack**: multi-call LLM inference, live web/corpus retrieval, per-claim ephemeral code execution, and a **human SME** on the critical path. The PRD is unusually good at *single-instance* failure honesty (VERIFY-15, ADMIN-23, ANALYTICS-20 all degrade truthfully). What it has almost nothing on is **behavior under concurrency and volume**: there is no capacity model, no throughput budget for the human bottleneck, no re-verification-storm control, no cross-consumer consistency bus, and no compute isolation between tenants. The product is specified as if one topic is verified at a time by an infinitely fast pipeline and an always-available SME. At 10k concurrent topic creations, a model rollout, or a 5,000-seat live event, the invariants and the latency budget fight each other and the invariants must win — which means **the system's realistic failure mode at scale is "correct but unavailable," and nothing in the doc bounds how unavailable.**

Ranked highest-leverage first. Tags: *[Blocker]* = the system does not stay correct-and-available at scale without it; *[High]* = a real outage/stale-trust/cost-cliff risk; *[Medium]* = real but survivable; *[Opportunity]* = net-new leverage.

---

### (a) Missing requirements / workflows / roles / rules / validation

- **[Blocker] No verification-capacity / admission-control / priority model — the "usually ready in under a minute" budget (VERIFY §Latency) is undefended under load.** The pipeline is many sequential LLM calls + retrieval + sandbox, per topic, and hard mode multiplies it. ADMIN-02 gives *observability* (queue depth, p50/p95) and ADMIN-23 gives a *"verification delayed"* state, but there is **no admission control, no per-tenant/per-user concurrency quota, no queue prioritization, and no honest ETA.** Critically, **LEARNER-EXAM's entire value proposition is a deadline**, yet there is no deadline-aware priority lane — an exam-prepper racing a date sits in the same FIFO as a casual browser and a Teams admin bulk-seeding 200 library topics. Missing: an SLO (not a hope), a priority-class model (deadline / interactive / batch-seed / guest-demo), admission control that sheds or queues *before* accepting work, and a predicted-ready ETA on the delayed state. Without this the differentiating "watch it run" experience degrades to "watch it wait," silently, exactly when the product is succeeding.

- **[Blocker] The SME-REVIEWER is a human-throughput single point of failure on the critical path of the entire trust guarantee, with no queue SLO, triage, or overflow design.** Every "freeze / route-to-SME" path converges on one human role: publish/assign gate (ORG-18), source promotion (COMM/ADMIN-10), Conflict resolution, quarantine release (ADMIN-14), injection containment (ADMIN-17), post-rollout re-review (ADMIN-04), cert-fraud review (ADMIN-15). ADMIN-14 admits the queue can "back up" and adds an *aging alert* — i.e., it surfaces the SPOF rather than solving it. **There is no defined SME turnaround SLA, no per-tenant/priority queue, no batch-adjudication or sample-and-clear tooling, and no overflow policy.** Consequences that are all currently unbounded: Teams topics stuck "needs SME" (blocking a paying tenant's time-to-value), quarantined claims stuck in limbo, certs blocked mid-issue. **The availability of the honesty promise is bounded by human review rate, and that bound is unmodeled.** Missing: an SME queue as a first-class, SLO'd, prioritized, tenant-fair subsystem with batch tooling and an explicit overflow behavior (what a topic's assignability *is* while stuck).

- **[Blocker] No re-verification-storm control. Model rollout, source retraction/promotion, and quarantine trigger unbounded recompute + unbounded SME fan-out.** ADMIN-04 says a Skeptic/model change "can flip trust states across the whole ledger" and VERIFY-17 promises "targeted re-match." But neither bounds the *scale* of the cascade: a judge change ramps across **every topic ever verified**; retracting/editing one shared source invalidates **every claim that cited it across every topic and tenant**; both simultaneously spike the compute plane *and* the SME queue (overturned Verified claims → Conflicts → SME) *and* the cert-review queue (ADMIN-04/15). Missing: a **claim→source and source→claim lineage graph** as a queryable index; a **bounded, prioritized re-verification scheduler** (rate-limited, batched, deferrable); and a defined "mixed-version ledger" contract for the window while a fleet-wide re-verify drains (which version is authoritative, what the trust bar shows). Today this is specified as a targeted operation but is architecturally a fleet-wide recompute with no backpressure.

- **[High] No canonical/shared verified-claim store — throughput ceiling is O(users × topics) of maximally-redundant LLM+retrieval+sandbox work.** Topics are per-user free text (VERIFY-01); two learners on "Dijkstra's algorithm" trigger two full independent pipelines. The business lens flagged the *cost*; the **reliability** consequence is distinct and worse: the system has **no relief valve on throughput** — every unit of demand is paid in full, so the pipeline melts under exactly the load that means the product is winning. Missing: claim/topic canonicalization + a content-addressable verified-ledger cache so recurring claim sets serve an existing ledger and run only incremental Skeptic/re-verify. This is a *scalability* requirement, not just a margin one.

- **[High] No ledger-change event bus / invalidation contract — every derived surface reimplements consistency, and a stale "Verified" is a lie.** The ledger is the source of truth and it is denormalized/cached everywhere: community verified-answer markers (COMM-18, "live not cached"), the Teams publish/assign gate (ORG-08/18, "reads live at publish time"), tests' exclude-Disputed set, Analytics aggregates, certificates, the Gap Map. Each domain independently promises to "read live" or "withdraw the marker." **There is no unified ledger-change stream with a defined propagation SLO to all consumers**, so stale-trust bugs live in the gaps between these ad-hoc implementations, and fan-out on a *hot* claim (a popular Disputed claim anchored by thousands of threads/answers) has no described model. Missing: a canonical ledger-change/invalidation event with a bounded propagation guarantee that every derived surface subscribes to, plus a defined staleness bound the product will tolerate.

- **[High] Data isolation ≠ compute isolation: multi-tenant Teams has no per-tenant compute quota / QoS, and no data-residency enforcement.** ADMIN-01/ORG §invariants specify hard *row-level* tenant data isolation (good), but the *verification compute plane is shared* and unquota'd. An ORG-ADMIN seeding a shared library "at scale," an EVENT-HOST running live sandbox for a big audience, or a guest-demo scraping attack (VERIFY-22 explicitly acknowledges "scraping the pipeline for free compute") **starves the shared pipeline for every paying user** — the classic noisy-neighbor failure, undefended. Separately, ADMIN-01 records a tenant `region` but **no residency enforcement** is specified across the LLM calls, retrieval corpora, or sandbox — table stakes for the named FERPA/GDPR "schools, bootcamps, regulated employers" buyers. Missing: per-tenant compute quotas/QoS classes, guest-demo isolation from the paid plane, and end-to-end residency pinning.

- **[High] No LLM-provider failover / rate-limit-survival design — the whole pipeline is a single-inference-dependency SPOF.** Every stage (Teach, Decompose, Verify, Skeptic, hard mode) is model inference. ADMIN-23's "honest degradation" tells the user verification is delayed but does **nothing to survive** a provider outage, a wave of provider 429s, or a model deprecation — a provider incident is a *total* verification outage. Missing: multi-provider/model abstraction, provider-level circuit-breaking and backoff, and a defined behavior when primary inference is unavailable (degrade to a slower fallback model *with a version tag*, not fabricate).

- **[Medium] No cross-topic search over the learner's own content; community search index has no reindex-on-visibility-change contract.** COMM-22 specifies access-scoped full-text thread search that "never leaks inaccessible content" and "degrades under index lag" — but unshare (ORG-09) and offboarding (ORG-14) *revoke* access, and there is no requirement that the search index reflect revocation promptly, which is a leak. And for LEARNER-POWER ("large portfolio over months") there is **no search across their own topics/claims/gaps at all** — no way to query "all my Unsupported claims" or "every Disputed claim in my library." Missing: an ACL-aware index with a reindex-on-ACL-change guarantee, and a cross-topic ledger/library search surface.

- **[Medium] No storage retention/tiering model for the write-heavy trust artifacts.** Sandbox traces + env fingerprints (per Verified·execution claim, VERIFY-12), the immutable audit log (ADMIN-20), coverage-matrix cells, per-topic model-version tags, and permanently-retained certificate records (ADMIN-21) all grow unbounded and are never given a retention/tiering/archival policy. Missing: retention tiers for reproducibility traces, audit-log compaction/archival, and a defined "how long must a Verified·execution stay re-runnable."

### (b) Risks & failure modes

- **[Blocker] Correct-but-unavailable is the realistic scale failure, and it cascades.** Because the invariants forbid shortcuts ("we'd rather stop than guess"), the only pressure-relief valve under overload is *latency*, and latency has no ceiling. A topic-creation surge backs up the pipeline → learners wait → the same overload hits the Conflict/SME queue → Teams publish gates stall → certs can't mint → live events (which reserve no capacity) fail their live verification. One capacity event propagates into every dependent surface because they all share the pipeline and the SME. There is no bulkhead between these blast zones.

- **[High] Model rollout is a coordinated multi-system storm, not a routine deploy.** A single Skeptic-tuning ramp (ADMIN-04) simultaneously: re-verifies the fleet (compute storm), overturns some Verified claims into Conflicts (SME storm), and flags every cert built on those claims for review (cert-review storm). ADMIN-04 requires T&S/SME co-sign and precision/recall guardrails — good governance — but **no throughput plan for the three queues the ramp itself detonates.** The safest change to the judge is operationally the most dangerous event the platform can run, and it's specified as a feature-flag flip.

- **[High] Sandbox is a cost/latency cliff with no pooling or result caching.** VERIFY-12/ADMIN-03 require every computational claim to run in a fresh, network-isolated, ephemeral environment. A topic with 40 computational claims = 40 cold-start environments; live events run them for concurrent viewers (EVENT §AI cost). The env *fingerprint* already exists for reproducibility (ADMIN-03) but is **not used as a cache key** — identical code + identical fingerprint re-executes every time. Under load this is where the compute bill and the p95 both explode, and the kill-switch/quarantine machinery (ADMIN-06) that halts a *job class* on a suspected escape can take down a large swath of in-flight execution at once with no graceful drain described.

- **[High] The learner-facing *read path* has no degraded mode — and the read path is the whole product.** Every degradation story (VERIFY-15, ADMIN-23) protects the *write/verify* path. But rendering a lecture *is* rendering trust states (underlines, ledger rail, live markers); if the trust-store read path is slow or partially unavailable, there is no defined behavior. You cannot show the lecture without the ledger, and you must not show it *wrong*. ANALYTICS-20 already solved this pattern for signals ("showing values as of <timestamp>") — but that pattern is **not applied to the lecture/ledger read path**, the highest-traffic surface. Under a ledger-store hiccup the honest options are "block the lecture" or "serve last-known-good with an as-of stamp," and neither is specified.

- **[High] Fail-closed audit (ADMIN-20) makes the audit sink a hard availability dependency during exactly the incidents you need to act.** Privileged actions "block or buffer" if the audit sink is down. During a live integrity incident (injection wave, escape, bad rollout) the audit store is itself under stress; fail-closed then blocks containment. "Buffered-then-flushed" is named but its ordering/durability guarantee under a sink outage isn't designed — buffer loss = unlogged privileged action, buffer-blocks = can't contain. This needs an explicit, incident-survivable accountability model, not a one-liner.

- **[Medium] Cert validity has ambiguous snapshot-vs-live semantics, which sets the cert-review fan-out unbounded.** ADMIN-15 makes revocation *live and propagated*, while ADMIN-04 says a model change "cannot retroactively invalidate an honestly-earned credential" yet must "surface those certs for review." So is a cert's validity a **snapshot** of claim states at issue-time, or a **live function** of current claim states? If live, every ledger change re-evaluates every dependent cert (fan-out storm + constant re-review). If snapshot, the "flag for review on re-flag" path needs an explicit, bounded trigger. Unspecified — and the choice determines whether a routine rollout floods the cert-review queue.

- **[Medium] Partial-verification reading collides with hard gates.** ADMIN-23 promises learners can read "already-verified portions with the unverified remainder marked." But the lecture is continuous prose with a **hard close-gate that locks "Next"** (LECTURE). Serving §1–2 verified while §3 is unverified means the learner either hits a locked gate with no verified next section (blocked) or is advanced into unverified content (invariant violation). The content model needs per-section verification state and a defined gate behavior at the verified/unverified boundary; today the two features contradict.

- **[Medium] Offline tolerance exists only for Review (REVIEW-17). The lecture, tests, and conflicts have no offline/interruption model** — a mobile learner mid-lecture or mid-test on a flaky connection has no defined graceful state on the surfaces that gate certificates.

### (c) Concrete improvements

- **[Blocker → fix] Stand up a verification-scheduler subsystem with QoS classes, admission control, and an honest ETA.** Priority classes: *interactive-deadline* (LEARNER-EXAM, Pro), *interactive* (topic creation), *batch* (Teams library seeding), *demo* (guests, hard-isolated). Admit-or-shed at intake with per-tenant/per-user concurrency quotas; make the "verification delayed" state carry a real predicted-ready time from live queue depth. *Impact:* bounds the core-promise latency under load, gives the deadline persona a lane that aligns with monetization, and firewalls guest/demo scraping off the paid plane.

- **[Blocker → fix] Model the SME queue as an SLO'd, prioritized, tenant-fair service with batch tooling.** Give it a turnaround SLA per priority/tier, tenant fairness, sample-and-clear + batch-adjudication tooling (essential for post-rollout and injection-wave spikes), and a defined overflow contract ("assignable-pending" semantics while stuck). *Impact:* converts the human bottleneck from an unbounded SPOF into a capacity-planned, degradable subsystem — the single biggest availability lever in the product.

- **[Blocker → fix] Build a claim/source lineage graph and a bounded re-verification scheduler.** Persist claim↔source dependency edges as a queryable index; feed all cascades (rollout, source retract/promote, quarantine) through a rate-limited, prioritized, resumable re-verify job with a defined mixed-version-ledger contract during drain. *Impact:* turns fleet-wide storms into throttled background drains, makes ADMIN-06 blast-radius computation a graph query instead of a scan, and keeps the compute + SME + cert queues from detonating together.

- **[High → fix] Introduce a canonical verified-ledger cache + content-addressable execution cache.** Canonicalize claims; serve an existing ledger for overlapping claim sets and run only incremental Skeptic. Key sandbox results on `(code, env-fingerprint)` — the fingerprint already exists (ADMIN-03), just make it a cache key. *Impact:* moves throughput from O(users×topics) toward O(unique claims), and turns the sandbox from a cold-start cliff into a warm-pool + cache. This is the throughput relief valve *and* the compounding data asset.

- **[High → fix] Define one ledger-change event stream every derived surface subscribes to, with a stated propagation SLO.** Replace the per-domain "read live"/"withdraw marker" promises (COMM-18, ORG-08/18, tests, Analytics, certs, Gap Map) with a single invalidation bus and an explicit tolerated-staleness bound. *Impact:* eliminates the class of stale-"Verified" bugs — which in this product are not bugs but *lies* — and gives hot-claim fan-out one place to be engineered.

- **[High → fix] Apply the ANALYTICS-20 "as-of" pattern to the lecture/ledger read path, and add provider failover.** On ledger-read degradation, serve last-known-good trust states stamped with an "as of" time rather than blocking or guessing; abstract inference behind a multi-provider layer with circuit-breaking so a provider outage degrades to a slower, version-tagged fallback instead of a full stop. *Impact:* keeps the highest-traffic surface truthful-and-available and removes the single-provider total-outage SPOF.

- **[Medium → fix] Add per-tenant compute quotas + residency pinning, and an ACL-change reindex guarantee for search.** Quota/QoS the shared pipeline per tenant; pin LLM/retrieval/sandbox to the tenant's `region`; require the community search index to drop revoked content within a bounded window on unshare/offboard, and add a cross-topic ledger search for the power persona. *Impact:* closes the noisy-neighbor and residency gaps that block regulated Teams buyers, and the access-leak in search.

### (d) Differentiators / opportunities

- **[Opportunity] Publish a live Ledger-Integrity SLO dashboard as a *public* trust signal.** The product's success metrics — Verified-later-overturned rate, SME-dismissed-Dispute rate, source-hallucination catch rate, sandbox flake rate (named across VERIFY/ADMIN/SKEPTIC-AI success criteria) — are today only measured at rollout time. Operate them continuously with canary/golden-set topics run through the *live* pipeline, and **publish the headline numbers.** No competitor can show "here is how often our verifier is wrong, measured in production." This turns an observability requirement into the strongest possible expression of the honesty brand.

- **[Opportunity] The canonical verified-corpus is simultaneously the scale relief valve and the moat.** A cross-tenant *canonical/public* verified-ledger layer (with tenant-private overlays for custom topics) is what lets the platform serve a claim it has already proven without re-running the pipeline. It compounds: every verification makes the next cheaper and faster. This is the one architecture choice that makes the trust ledger a *data* moat rather than a UX moat — and it's a scalability decision, not a business one.

- **[Opportunity] Sell the deadline priority lane and reserved live-event capacity as entitlements.** A guaranteed-fast verification lane (LEARNER-EXAM) and pre-provisioned live-verification capacity for large scheduled events (EVENT-HOST) are natural paid capabilities that *also* solve the capacity-planning problem — the users who most stress the system are the ones who will pay to reserve against it. Monetization and capacity management become the same lever.

- **[Opportunity] Content-addressable execution results as a shareable proof artifact.** A `(code, env-fingerprint) → verdict + trace` cache is not just an internal optimization — a reproducible, cacheable Verified·execution proof is itself a portable, verifiable artifact (bound by EXEC-SANDBOX's envelope) that DEVELOPER-API consumers and EMPLOYER-VERIFIER could re-run. The reliability optimization and a differentiated external payload are the same object.

---

**Bottom line for downstream:** VeriLearn's failure honesty is excellent *per instance* and nearly absent *under load* — there is no capacity model, no throughput budget for the human SME on the critical path, no control over re-verification cascades, no cross-consumer consistency bus, and no compute isolation between tenants. The three load-bearing fixes are a **verification scheduler with QoS/admission control**, the **SME queue and re-verification cascade turned into SLO'd, bounded, lineage-driven subsystems**, and a **canonical ledger + execution cache** that both relieves throughput and compounds into a moat. Ship those and the invariants stay correct-and-available as the product scales; skip them and VeriLearn is a system whose correctness guarantees survive scale only by becoming slower and slower until, precisely when it is most successful, it is honest but unusable.
