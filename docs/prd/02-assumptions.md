## Key Assumptions & Open Decisions

VeriLearn is a **trust product**, and a trust product is a stack of bets. Every headline claim — "nothing unverified ships," "four honest signals," "a credential an employer trusts," "$12/mo unlimited" — rests on an assumption that is currently *asserted, not validated*. The completeness review flagged this register as the one missing artifact precisely because the PRD reasons brilliantly about mechanics while leaving the load-bearing premises implicit. That is dangerous: the product's own thesis is that a confident, unchecked assertion is a liability, and an un-surfaced assumption is exactly that.

Each row below is a bet the product's viability depends on. If a **Blocker**-impact assumption is false, the thesis (or the company) does not survive contact with reality; **High** means large money, defensibility, or trust is lost but the product limps on; **Medium** means real but survivable damage. Confidence is our honest prior *today*, not a wish. The final column is the cheap experiment that buys down the risk **before** we bet the roadmap on it — nearly always cheaper than discovering the assumption was wrong after launch, in a market, in a lawsuit, or in a viral tweet. Treat any **Low-confidence / Blocker** row as a de-risking task that should precede or run concurrent with R1, not a footnote.

### Open Decisions

The top decisions that must be made before, or early in, the build. Each blocks or shapes multiple assumptions above; leaving them implicit is how the register got missed in the first place.

- **OD-1 — Credential independence model (self-signed certs).** *(ASM-17, ASM-20)*
  - Options: **(a)** issue self-authored, self-verified certs unlabeled (status quo — the learner authors, verifies, adjudicates, sources, and names their own credential); **(b)** a visible, honestly-labeled **independence ladder** (self-study → peer-reviewed → SME-verified → proctored & identity-verified) surfaced on the verify page; **(c)** route every cert through an independent examination path.
  - **Recommended default: (b).** Ship self-study certs in R1 *labeled as such* ("self-verified — no independent review") so honesty matches marketing; build higher tiers as the Teams/enterprise monetization path. (b) turns the biggest integrity weakness into the product's most on-brand feature and rescues the certs that deserve to mean something from the ones that don't.

- **OD-2 — Cost governance under Pro "unlimited."** *(ASM-15, ASM-16)*
  - Options: **(a)** genuinely unlimited (status quo); **(b)** keep "unlimited topics" as marketing but **meter the expensive levers** — hard-mode Skeptic and thorough re-verification — with a generous monthly credit pool + paid overage (or a Power/Pro-Plus tier); **(c)** a hard per-account cap.
  - **Recommended default: (b).** It caps the loss-making accounts exactly where cost lives, creates a natural expansion-revenue lever, and preserves the headline. Pair with the ASM-16 canonical-ledger cache as the structural fix.

- **OD-3 — SME sourcing and economics.** *(ASM-18, ASM-19, ASM-20)*
  - Options: **(a)** staffed SMEs (COGS scales linearly with content); **(b)** community/volunteer reviewers; **(c)** a **paid SME marketplace** with reputation staking; **(d)** an automated cross-model **adversarial re-examiner** as first line, with a sampled human SME audit behind it.
  - **Recommended default: (d) + (c) hybrid.** Use an independent-model re-examiner to make self-directed certs mean something without a human in every loop, backed by a sampled paid-marketplace SME audit; gate the heavy human tier to R3 where Teams/credential-independence demand it. Model the queue as an SLO'd, tenant-fair, prioritized subsystem regardless.

- **OD-4 — Verifier abstention + domain-risk gating.** *(ASM-01, ASM-02)*
  - Options: **(a)** five states, the verifier always answers (status quo); **(b)** add a **`Needs human review`** abstention state triggered by low confidence / thin sources, plus a domain-risk policy that forces expert review (or refusal) for enumerated high-stakes domains (medicine, law, safety) **regardless of plan**.
  - **Recommended default: (b).** A verifier that publicly says "I don't know — a human must" is *more* trustworthy than one that always answers, and it prevents the highest-consequence confidently-wrong outputs. Turn model uncertainty into a visible honesty feature.

- **OD-5 — Review-due reminder default.** *(ASM-14)*
  - Options: **(a)** off by default (the implied status quo — the retention engine's only recurring trigger ships silent); **(b)** a **default-on, honest, single daily review nudge** for consenting adult accounts with due cards, one-tap suppressible, quiet-hours-respecting; minors and consent-indeterminate accounts stay off.
  - **Recommended default: (b),** documented explicitly as a deliberate honesty-compatible choice. An honest, suppressible nudge to do the thing the learner signed up for is not a dark pattern; leaving the FSRS heartbeat off-by-default wastes the retention investment for everyone who never finds the toggle.

- **OD-6 — Model-update / re-verification policy.** *(ASM-07)*
  - Options: **(a)** freeze verdicts at creation-time forever (silent stale truth); **(b)** auto-refresh on every rollout (mass cert-revocation storms); **(c)** a **staleness lifecycle** — version-stamp each topic, mark affected topics stale on rollout, re-verify on a governed/throttled schedule, show the learner a verdict-diff, and route any cert-bearing downgrade through the revocation-materiality check with notice.
  - **Recommended default: (c),** tied to the ASM-01 eval gate (a rollout that regresses the gold set never ships; one that improves it triggers prioritized re-verification).

- **OD-7 — Test integrity / assurance tier.** *(ASM-24)*
  - Options: **(a)** unproctored only, but **disclose the assurance tier** on every cert and verify page; **(b)** add an optional **identity-verified + proctored** tier as an enterprise/Teams upsell; **(c)** require proctoring for all cert-bearing (Mastery) tests.
  - **Recommended default: (a) now, (b) in R3.** Disclosure is cheap and ships immediately (unproctored is fine; *undisclosed* unproctored dressed as a trust credential is not). The proctored/identity tier is the value-creating, sellable upgrade and the natural enterprise motion. Also secure the item bank (held-out items, suppress exact-claim disclosure until attempts exhausted).

- **OD-8 — "Transfer" signal: relabel vs. build the bank.** *(ASM-11)*
  - Options: **(a)** keep calling the taught-set-derived signal "Transfer"; **(b)** **rename it "Application"** now and only reintroduce "Transfer" once a validated far-transfer item bank exists; **(c)** build the far-transfer bank immediately as a moat.
  - **Recommended default: (b) immediately, (c) as a funded differentiator.** Renaming is free and closes an overclaim the product's own Skeptic would flag in a competitor; the far-transfer bank (novel-context, multi-claim items) is expensive, which is exactly why it becomes the only honest transfer signal in ed-tech if built.
