## Security, Privacy Engineering & Compliance (GDPR / FERPA / COPPA)

### Overview

This domain owns the **controls that keep VeriLearn lawful and trustworthy as a custodian of personal data** — distinct from, and orthogonal to, the epistemic firewall that keeps the *ledger* honest. Every other domain has been careful about "who can decree a claim `Verified`." This domain answers the different, equally load-bearing question: *where does a learner's — possibly a minor's — free-text physically go, who processes it, under what agreement, how is it protected, and how is it deleted?* If the trust spine is VeriLearn's moat, this domain is the ground the moat sits on: without encryption, tenant isolation, breach response, sub-processor DPAs, and data-subject rights, no school, no EU buyer, and no privacy-conscious learner can lawfully use the product regardless of how honest the ledger is.

Four structural facts make security and privacy foundational rather than incidental here:

1. **Learner-authored free-text is everywhere and is high-entropy PII.** Topic specs, predict/connection/cloze responses, rubric-graded task answers, Skeptic threads, and community posts are all *free-text the learner types*. A learner can — and will — paste their own name, a classmate's name, health details, or an employer's confidential material into a task answer. That text is not only stored; it is **transmitted to third-party LLM inference providers** (the engines behind the Pipeline, Skeptic, and grader). The Privacy panel's binding promise (*"we never sell your data or train public models on it"*) is about VeriLearn's own models; it is silent on the sub-processors that actually receive the raw text. Closing that gap — a sub-processor register, zero-retention inference agreements, and a PII-redaction pass before text leaves the trust boundary — is the single highest-leverage obligation in this domain.
2. **Minors are in the marketed audience.** GUARDIAN is a named persona ("typically a teen"), and exam-prep students are frequently high-schoolers. That triggers **COPPA** (US under-13), **FERPA** (US education records once a school assigns the product), and GDPR's child-consent regime the moment any personal data is collected. Age handling therefore cannot be "Future"; a hard age-gate and minor-safe defaults are foundational.
3. **Multi-tenancy makes isolation a safety property, not hygiene.** Honest signals (what a learner got wrong, where they are overconfident) are genuinely sensitive behavioral data. One org's learners, signals, or identities leaking into another's is a breach, not a bug — so isolation must hold at the crypto and authorization layer, fail-closed, and be verifiable.
4. **The product runs untrusted code and untrusted prompts by design.** The Execution Sandbox runs machine-generated code to mint `Verified·execution`, and every surface renders untrusted free-text (learner- *and* LLM-authored). These are the two largest technical attack surfaces in the product; their security envelopes and prompt-injection/exfiltration defenses are owned here as requirements even though their *operation* lives in the Pipeline/Sandbox/Platform-Admin domains.

This domain **specifies controls and governs compliance**; it does not re-own the surfaces those controls protect. MFA mechanics live in Auth; the DSAR self-service export UI lives in Settings; tenant provisioning and the sandbox's runtime live in Platform-Admin; RBAC role definitions live in Org-Admin; the certificate verify page lives in Tests. This domain states the *requirement, threat model, invariant, and legal basis* those domains must satisfy, and owns the cross-cutting machinery — breach response, sub-processor governance, retention schedules, data-residency, audit tamper-evidence, and the data-subject-rights backbone — that has no other home.

### Personas involved

- **Compliance / Data-Protection Officer (COMPLIANCE-DPO)** — the primary steward of this domain. Owns lawful-basis mapping, the sub-processor register and DPAs, retention schedules, DSAR/erasure fulfillment governance, the breach-notification clock, FERPA/COPPA posture, and data-residency policy. Governs *data*, never trust states.
- **Trust & Safety / Ledger Integrity Lead (TRUST-SAFETY-LEAD)** — owns audit-log integrity and trust-ledger tamper-evidence, insider-threat controls over epistemic-write roles, incident containment (freeze, revoke), and forensics. Protects the ledger's integrity; cannot hand-certify.
- **Platform Administrator (PLATFORM-ADMIN)** — vendor-side root operator who *implements* the isolation, encryption, sandbox envelope, and break-glass controls this domain specifies; operates infrastructure but can never read or alter epistemic content.
- **Organization / Teams Admin (ORG-ADMIN)** — the buyer-side counterparty for the contractual layer: accepts the DPA, elects data region, acknowledges sub-processors, sets the FERPA designation, and mandates MFA for the tenant. Consumes tenant-scoped breach notifications.
- **Parent / Guardian (GUARDIAN)** — the consent authority for a minor dependent: satisfies the COPPA age-gate, provides verifiable parental consent, and holds minor-safe visibility controls. Cannot certify or alter trust states.
- **Every learner, as a data subject (LEARNER-SELF, LEARNER-EXAM, LEARNER-POWER, LEARNER-SKEPTIC, LEARNER-TEAM, A11Y-LEARNER)** — the natural person whose rights (access, portability, rectification, erasure, consent, objection to automated decisions) this domain exists to honor. Every learner is a data subject first, whatever their learning persona.
- **Skeptical / Expert Learner (LEARNER-SKEPTIC)** — additionally a stress-test: expects the data-conduct claims to be as provable as the epistemic ones (sub-processor transparency, no-train attestation).
- **Support Agent (SUPPORT-AGENT)** — acts on accounts under scoped, logged consent; a *watched* insider whose access to plaintext learner content must be firewalled, minimized, and audited.
- **Employer / Recruiter (EMPLOYER-VERIFIER)** — external consumer of certificates whose reliance interest is why erasure and certificate revocation are decoupled, and why the verify page must resist enumeration/harvesting.
- **Developer / API & Integration Consumer (DEVELOPER-API)** — bound by scoped tokens, rate limits, and the rule that integrations never mutate the ledger or bypass consent/residency controls.
- **The Skeptic (SKEPTIC-AI) & Verification Pipeline (VERIFY-PIPELINE)** — system principals that process untrusted learner text and untrusted retrieved sources; subject to prompt-injection/exfiltration defenses and sub-processor data-flow constraints.
- **Execution Sandbox (EXEC-SANDBOX)** — the system principal that runs untrusted machine-generated code; subject to the strictest isolation/egress/resource envelope in the product.

### User stories

- **[SEC-01] As a Trust & Safety / Ledger Integrity Lead (TRUST-SAFETY-LEAD), I want phishing-resistant MFA and step-up re-authentication mandated for every privileged and epistemic-write role, so that a single compromised credential can never mint trust into a certificate an employer relies on.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* SME-REVIEWER and other epistemic-write accounts are the most valuable insider-threat target in the product; the entire "un-buyable ledger" claim collapses if one of them is phished.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - Epistemic-write roles (SME-REVIEWER, moderators with promotion power) and vendor roles (PLATFORM-ADMIN, TRUST-SAFETY-LEAD, SUPPORT-AGENT) must enroll a phishing-resistant factor (WebAuthn/passkey or hardware security key); TOTP alone is insufficient for these roles.
    - Certifications underlying certificate-eligible topics require **dual-control (2-of-N)** approval and per-action step-up re-authentication.
    - Anomalous certification/action patterns (rate spikes, off-hours bursts) raise a signal to TRUST-SAFETY-LEAD.
  - *Business rules / validation:* The mechanics of MFA enrollment and step-up are owned by Auth (AUTH-11); this story adds the *policy* that certain roles cannot opt out and certain actions cannot be single-signed. Disabling a phishing-resistant factor on a mandated account is blocked. Dual-control cannot be satisfied by two sessions of the same principal.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* Lost hardware key follows an identity-verified recovery path that itself requires a second reviewer, never a self-serve reset. If only one SME is available and dual-control cannot be met, the certification is *queued* (topic remains non-certificate-eligible) rather than downgraded to single-control. A11Y-LEARNER-operable authenticators (AUTH-22) must satisfy the phishing-resistant requirement.

- **[SEC-02] As a Platform Administrator (PLATFORM-ADMIN), I want least-privilege RBAC that enforces the administrative-versus-epistemic firewall at the authorization layer, so that access to a surface never silently grants power to decree truth.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* The firewall is only real if it is enforced in code at every write, not merely asserted in role descriptions.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - Every trust-state write, Conflict resolution, source promotion, and certificate issuance is gated on an explicit epistemic-write capability that no administrative, billing, support, or infrastructure role possesses.
    - Authorization is **fail-closed**: an unrecognized role, an expired scope, or a missing tenant context denies the action rather than defaulting to allow.
    - Separation-of-duties is enforced: the principal who authors content cannot be the sole principal who certifies it; the principal who investigates cannot unilaterally erase the evidence.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Role definitions live in Org-Admin (tenant roles) and Platform-Admin (vendor roles); this story owns the invariant that capabilities are *additive and least-privilege*, never inherited by convenience. Break-glass elevation (PLATFORM-ADMIN) is time-boxed, reason-required, and cannot include an epistemic-write capability.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A role-mapping bug that would grant epistemic-write to an admin must fail closed and alarm, not fail open. A revoked capability takes effect on the next request (no waiting for token expiry) for high-stakes actions. Attempting an epistemic write with an administrative token is denied *and* audited as a firewall-violation attempt.

- **[SEC-03] As an Organization / Teams Admin (ORG-ADMIN), I want hard multi-tenant isolation enforced down to the cryptographic layer, so that no query, log, export, or model call can carry one org's learners, signals, or identities into another.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* Isolation is the precondition of every Teams promise and every privacy claim; a cross-tenant leak is a reportable breach for two customers at once.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - Every entity carries a tenant boundary; reads and writes are tenant-scoped with fail-closed authorization, and cross-tenant access is impossible even for a query missing its tenant predicate (default-deny, not default-all).
    - Teams tenants get **per-tenant key isolation** (envelope encryption with a tenant-scoped key) so isolation holds at rest cryptographically, not only via row scoping.
    - Sub-processor (LLM) calls carry tenant context and cannot batch one tenant's text into another's request or cache.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Tenant provisioning and the isolation boundary are established in Platform-Admin (ADMIN-01) and Auth (AUTH-20); this story owns the crypto-layer requirement and the automated isolation test that must pass continuously. Logs, backups, and analytics inherit the same boundary.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A confirmed cross-tenant read triggers SEC-17 (breach) for every affected tenant. A shared cache or embedding index must be tenant-partitioned; a global index that could surface one tenant's content to another is prohibited. Vendor break-glass into a tenant is logged to that tenant's audit and cannot read epistemic content.

- **[SEC-04] As a Compliance / Data-Protection Officer (COMPLIANCE-DPO), I want a non-negotiable encryption baseline in transit and at rest with managed key rotation, so that a storage-layer compromise never exposes plaintext education records or behavioral profiles.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* Encryption "where feasible" fails every enterprise/education security questionnaire on day one and leaves the most sensitive data — honest signals and free-text — unprotected.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - TLS 1.2+ (prefer 1.3) for all data in transit, including internal service-to-service and every sub-processor call; no plaintext transport.
    - AES-256 (or equivalent) at rest for all learner data classes — ledger, honest signals, free-text answers, identity, credentials, billing tokens, backups.
    - Envelope encryption with a managed KMS, documented key-rotation schedule, and separation between key-management and data-plane access.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Credentials are hashed with a memory-hard function (owned by Auth); this story owns the at-rest/in-transit/KMS baseline everything else inherits. Payment data is tokenized, never stored raw (owned by Billing). Encryption status per data class is part of the SOC2/vendor-questionnaire evidence (SEC-22).
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A data class discovered to be stored unencrypted is treated as a control gap and remediated on a tracked clock; if it held PII and was exposed, SEC-17 applies. Key-rotation failure must alarm and block new writes to the affected store rather than silently continuing on a stale key. A lost/compromised tenant key triggers re-encryption and incident review.

- **[SEC-05] As a Learner (any persona, as a data subject), I want free-text I type to be scanned for personal data before it is stored or sent to any AI provider, so that I don't accidentally leak my own — or a named third party's — private information into a sub-processor's logs.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* Free-text answers are the highest-probability PII-leak path in the product, and the destination (third-party inference) is the hardest to claw back from.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A PII-detection/redaction gate runs on learner free-text **before persistence and before any LLM call**, flagging or masking obvious direct identifiers (names, emails, phone numbers, government IDs, addresses) and offering the learner a chance to redact when high-confidence PII is detected in a low-PII-expected field.
    - Redaction is applied to the copy sent to sub-processors; the learner's original submission is stored under the same encryption and retention rules as other content.
    - The gate degrades safely: if detection is uncertain it does not silently drop content, it warns or passes through per policy without blocking the learner's flow.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Detection is best-effort and advisory to the learner, not a guarantee; the binding controls are the sub-processor DPAs (SEC-06) and encryption (SEC-04). Third-party PII (the learner names a real person) is in scope: authorship anonymization on erasure does not scrub PII *in the text*, so redaction at intake is the primary mitigation. Grading and verification operate on the learner's real answer, not a lossy redaction, unless the learner chooses to redact.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* The gate must not block or corrupt legitimate technical content (a task answer that legitimately contains what looks like an identifier, e.g. a sample SSN in a data-privacy lesson). A detection-service outage fails to *pass-through with the existing DPA protections*, never to a silent hard-block that strands the learner mid-lecture. Redaction is never applied retroactively to already-verified claims' provenance.

- **[SEC-06] As a Compliance / Data-Protection Officer (COMPLIANCE-DPO), I want a first-class sub-processor register backed by zero-retention, no-train inference agreements, so that the honesty promise on the Privacy panel is literally true for every party that touches learner text.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* A verification-first brand marketed on honesty being caught overstating its own data handling is the single most damaging incident possible; it is also a GDPR Art. 28/30 and FERPA "school official" hard requirement.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A maintained sub-processor register (public page + in-app "where your data goes" disclosure) lists every party that processes learner data — model providers, hosting, email, analytics, payments — with purpose and region.
    - Every LLM/inference provider is under a contractual **zero-retention / no-train** agreement; providers without one cannot be routed learner text.
    - The Settings Privacy panel's "we never sell or train on your data" clause is extended and kept in sync to cover inference providers explicitly, not just VeriLearn's own models.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Adding a new sub-processor requires a DPA and register update before production traffic; material additions are surfaced to affected tenants and, where consent is the basis, to data subjects (Settings I4 sync). This story owns the register and DPA governance; the Privacy panel copy is rendered by Settings.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A provider that changes its terms to allow retention/training is removed from the routing pool until re-contracted or replaced. A fallback/emergency provider not on the register cannot be silently enabled. If the register and the actual data flow diverge, that is a compliance incident even absent external exposure.

- **[SEC-07] As a Compliance / Data-Protection Officer (COMPLIANCE-DPO), I want consent captured against an explicit lawful basis with a cookie/ePrivacy consent layer, so that every processing purpose is defensible and EU learners are not tracked without a legal ground.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* Required for EU operation and cheap to fix, but layers onto the existing analytics opt-out rather than blocking the core loop.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - An internal processing register maps each purpose (core service delivery, analytics, product email, community, model-quality evaluation) to a lawful basis (contract, consent, legitimate interest) with documented balancing tests where relevant.
    - A cookie/consent-management layer gates non-essential cookies and tracking for EU/UK visitors, with granular per-purpose choices and a reversible record.
    - Consent choices are versioned; withdrawing consent stops the corresponding processing going forward and is honored at the collection layer (per Settings I4), not merely at display.
  - *Business rules / validation:* The self-service toggles (analytics, product email, community visibility) are owned by Settings (SETTINGS-12); this story owns the lawful-basis mapping and the cookie/ePrivacy consent instrument. Transactional/security/legally-required communications are never gated on marketing consent.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A purpose without a mapped lawful basis cannot go live. If a consent record is lost or ambiguous, processing defaults to the most restrictive interpretation. A downstream consumer briefly unaware of a withdrawal still has events dropped at source (propagation lag does not permit continued collection).

- **[SEC-08] As a Learner (any persona, as a data subject), I want to formally request access to, a portable copy of, and correction of all personal data VeriLearn holds about me, so that I can exercise my GDPR/CCPA subject rights beyond the self-service export.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* A right-of-access and portability path is legally mandatory for a product holding learner (and potentially education/minor) records; the self-service export is a building block but not the full instrument.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A verified data subject can submit an access/portability/rectification request; the system verifies identity, locates the subject's full data footprint across topics, ledger, reviews, gaps, threads, calibration, certs, audit references, and billing, and produces a machine-readable package within a stated SLA (e.g. 30 days).
    - The portable export is **faithful** — it includes the full claim ledger with trust states, sources, confidence, and coverage matrix (per Settings I5), not prose stripped of its epistemics.
    - Rectification corrects identity/profile data and propagates it (name on future certificates, display name) without altering the immutable epistemic record.
  - *Business rules / validation:* The self-service export UI and format are owned by Settings (SETTINGS-13) and the DPO fulfillment workflow by Settings (SETTINGS-14); this story owns the *rights-request backbone* — intake, identity verification, SLA tracking, and cross-domain orchestration. Full-portfolio export requires step-up re-auth and a post-export notification; a Teams **seat** learner's export is scoped to their *own* data and excludes the org's shared-library ledger (org-IP protection). Rectifying an already-issued certificate's name is a controlled re-issue, not an in-place edit of a signed credential.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A very large portfolio streams/chunks rather than timing out; a partial failure issues no link and is retryable. An access request from an unverified requester is refused with a neutral, non-enumerating message. Rectification cannot be used to rewrite what a claim said or how it was graded (that is not "personal data about me").

- **[SEC-09] As a Learner (any persona, as a data subject), I want a right-to-erasure that truly cascades across every store while honoring legitimate retention, so that leaving VeriLearn means my personal data is gone — cleanly, not half-deleted.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* Erasure is a legal baseline and a trust signal, but it is genuinely hard to do correctly across ledger/reviews/gaps/community/audit without orphaning data or holing the ledger.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - Erasure enters a disclosed grace window, then hard-deletes and cascades personal data across topics, ledger, reviews, gaps, threads, calibration, and derived indices/embeddings, with atomic rollback on partial failure (no orphaned references).
    - **Certificate decoupling (Settings I7):** personal content is erased while a minimal, non-personal certificate verification record may be retained per legal basis and disclosed to the subject before deletion; revocation is a separate, explicit act.
    - **Community/ledger reconciliation:** promoted-into-source community content is canonicalized away from PII-bearing free-text (SME rewrites the promoted claim/source into neutral form) so erasure succeeds without breaking other learners' verified topics.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Deletion mechanics and confirmations are owned by Settings/Auth (SETTINGS-19, AUTH-19); this story owns the cross-store cascade contract, the retention/legal-hold reconciliation, and the audit pseudonymization rule (SEC-16). A **legal hold** or an active Trust & Safety investigation (freeze) overrides auto-purge and blocks erasure until lifted, with the conflict surfaced. A sole Teams/Org admin must transfer ownership before self-erasure.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* Erasure requested while a pipeline is running cancels the run cleanly, no zombie job. Backups are handled by a documented rolling-purge (deleted data ages out of backups within a stated window and is never restored into production). A minor's erasure additionally purges guardian-consent artifacts except where a legal basis requires retaining proof that consent existed.

- **[SEC-10] As a Compliance / Data-Protection Officer (COMPLIANCE-DPO), I want defined retention schedules per data class with automatic minimization of dormant data, so that "we honor retention" is auditable instead of aspirational.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* GDPR storage-limitation and FERPA both require defined, defensible periods; the schedules are asserted across the product but never enumerated.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A data-class inventory (identity, ledger, honest signals, free-text answers, community content, audit, certs, billing, consent records) each carries a default retention period and legal basis, applied automatically.
    - Soft-deleted data auto-purges after its grace window; dormant/inactive accounts are minimized or purged on a defined schedule after prior notice.
    - Retention schedules are configurable per data class and per tenant where a customer's regulatory regime requires a different period.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Certificate/audit retention reconciles with erasure via the pseudonymization rule (SEC-16) and cert decoupling (Settings I7). A legal hold overrides auto-purge. Retention changes are versioned and cannot silently shorten a period below a legally mandated floor (e.g. FERPA/financial minimums).
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A dormant-account purge sends advance notice and honors any active certificate's verification-record retention. A tenant offboarding triggers the tenant's contractually agreed data-return-then-delete sequence, not an immediate wipe. If a data class has no assigned retention, it defaults to the shortest defensible period and is flagged for DPO review, never "keep forever."

- **[SEC-11] As a Parent / Guardian (GUARDIAN), I want a hard age-gate at signup that blocks under-threshold accounts until verifiable consent exists, so that my child is never onboarded into unlawful data collection.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* The product markets to teens and to students; an under-13 self-declaring adult is a COPPA violation on the first byte of collected personal data, per-child. This must ship, not wait.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - Signup captures/derives an age band; an account below the COPPA threshold is **hard-blocked from processing personal data** until verifiable guardian consent is obtained (SEC-12).
    - Minor-safe defaults are enforced immediately: no public community profile, suppressed marketing/product email, no public certificate name page, and data minimization on collection.
    - If age is later corrected downward to a minor, the account is retroactively placed under the minor policy (community delisted, consent required) rather than continuing under adult defaults.
  - *Business rules / validation:* The signup age-gate surface is rendered by Auth (pulling AUTH-21's stated end-state forward from Future to MVP); this story owns the *policy* that under-threshold accounts cannot process personal data without consent and inherit minor-safe defaults (invariant SEC-I8). Self-declared age is not sufficient on its own for consent — verifiable guardian consent is required for the youngest band.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* An under-threshold learner cannot bypass the gate by editing their birthdate after the fact; downward corrections tighten, never loosen. If a school context is detected (FERPA, SEC-13), the school-authorization basis may substitute for individual guardian consent per the tenant's designation. A minor blocked pending consent still receives essential safety functions, not a broken app.

- **[SEC-12] As a Parent / Guardian (GUARDIAN), I want to provide verifiable parental consent and hold safety controls for a linked dependent, so that my child can use VeriLearn lawfully and I retain oversight.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* Turns the biggest regulatory liability into a lawful school/family wedge, but depends on a guardian-linked account model that is more than the MVP age-gate.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A guardian completes a verifiable-consent flow (stronger than a checkbox) that records who consented, to what scope, and when, and links guardian↔dependent accounts.
    - The guardian holds billing and safety controls (visibility, community exposure, data-sharing) for the dependent and can withdraw consent, which suspends non-essential processing going forward.
    - The guardian receives an honest disclosure of any current product limitations (e.g. interim single-shared-account behavior) rather than a hidden gap.
  - *Business rules / validation:* The guardian owns billing/safety but has **no epistemic authority** — cannot certify claims, resolve Conflicts, or alter trust states. Consent artifacts are retained per legal basis even through the dependent's later erasure where proof-of-consent is required. Withdrawal of consent triggers the minor-blocked-pending-consent state (SEC-11), not deletion of already-earned learning by default.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* Fraudulent or unverifiable consent attempts are rejected and audited. A dependent aging past the threshold transitions to a self-managed adult account with disclosure and a re-consent-for-continued-processing step. Loss of the guardian link (guardian account deleted) must not orphan the minor into an unconsented state — it re-enters pending-consent.

- **[SEC-13] As an Organization / Teams Admin (ORG-ADMIN), I want FERPA-compliant handling when my school assigns VeriLearn to students, so that student records are treated as education records under a valid school-official agreement.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* FERPA obligations trigger the moment a school assigns the product; without a school-official designation and education-record handling, no US school can lawfully adopt it.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A tenant can be designated a FERPA education context; within it, learner data is treated as an education record with the school as controller and VeriLearn as a "school official"/processor under written agreement.
    - Directory-information and disclosure rules are respected: no disclosure of student records outside the school-authorized scope, and no public certificate name page for minor students by default.
    - The school retains the right to inspect, correct, and direct deletion of its students' records, exercised through the ORG-ADMIN/DPO surfaces.
  - *Business rules / validation:* The DPO can constrain the tenant's visibility policy for regulated/minor tenants (references ORG-21); this story owns the FERPA designation, the school-official agreement instrument (via SEC-14), and education-record treatment. In a FERPA tenant, the school's authorization can substitute for individual COPPA guardian consent per the agreement. Automated-decision transparency (SEC-23) applies to any grade/readiness signal shared with the school.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A FERPA tenant cannot enable public community exposure or leaderboards for its minor students even if org policy would otherwise allow it (minor-safe defaults win). Offboarding a FERPA tenant returns records to the school before deletion. A student record requested for erasure while the school has a records-retention obligation resolves in favor of the mandated retention, disclosed to the subject.

- **[SEC-14] As an Organization / Teams Admin (ORG-ADMIN), I want a self-serve compliance-agreements surface (DPA, SCCs, sub-processor acknowledgment, region, FERPA designation), so that my legal review closes without a bespoke back-and-forth.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* Turns "legal will get back to you" into self-serve and shortens enterprise/education sales cycles; the contractual layer is legally required but not part of the core learning loop.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A tenant-level surface exposes: DPA acceptance (controller↔processor terms), Standard Contractual Clauses where transfers apply, sub-processor list acknowledgment (SEC-06), data-region election (SEC-15), and FERPA school-official designation (SEC-13).
    - Acceptance is recorded with signer, timestamp, and version; material changes to sub-processors or terms notify the tenant and, where required, pause new processing until re-acknowledged.
    - The surface is gated into Teams onboarding so a tenant cannot go live processing regulated data without the applicable instruments accepted.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Tenant onboarding and roster are owned by Org-Admin (ORG-01); this story owns the compliance-agreement records and their gating. Only an authorized tenant signer (ORG-ADMIN or a designated legal contact) can accept binding terms; a seat learner cannot. Region election is bounded by what VeriLearn actually supports (SEC-15).
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A tenant that declines a materially expanded sub-processor scope keeps the narrower prior scope or is offered an off-ramp, never silently upgraded. A missing required instrument blocks the corresponding processing (e.g. no SCCs → no cross-region transfer) rather than proceeding unlawfully.

- **[SEC-15] As a Compliance / Data-Protection Officer (COMPLIANCE-DPO), I want per-tenant data-region pinning covering storage and inference routing, so that EU/regulated learner data stays in-region and cross-border transfers carry lawful safeguards.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* Unlocks the EU/public-sector/education segment the Teams tier targets and closes GDPR Chapter V transfer exposure; compounds the sub-processor gap if left open.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A tenant elects a data region (EU/US at minimum); storage, backups, and **LLM inference routing** honor the election.
    - Where a transfer outside the elected region is unavoidable, it is covered by SCCs and a transfer-impact assessment; otherwise it is blocked.
    - Region status is visible in the tenant registry and in the "where your data goes" disclosure (SEC-06).
  - *Business rules / validation:* Region is a tenant-registry field established at provisioning (references ADMIN-01) but this story makes it a *guarantee* over storage and processing, not a label. Inference routing must select an in-region provider or a provider whose in-region processing is contractually assured; a provider without in-region capability cannot serve an EU-pinned tenant.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* An in-region provider outage does not silently fail over to an out-of-region one; it degrades (queue/retry) or uses a pre-agreed in-region fallback. A region change for an existing tenant migrates data with verification, not a lazy dual-region straddle. Free-tier individual learners default to a documented region with disclosure.

- **[SEC-16] As a Trust & Safety / Ledger Integrity Lead (TRUST-SAFETY-LEAD), I want a tamper-evident audit log and cryptographically integrity-checked trust ledger, so that no one — including an insider — can alter history or forge a Verified state without detection.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* Tamper-evidence is what makes the ledger's honesty *provable* rather than merely asserted, and the audit trail is the evidence source for every incident and DSAR.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - The audit log is append-only and tamper-evident (hash-chained / sequenced) so any retroactive edit or deletion is detectable; trust-state transitions carry an integrity check binding claim, state, source, and actor.
    - Audit entries record actor, scope, timestamp, legal/consent basis, and before/after state for every privileged, epistemic-write, and data-rights action.
    - **Pseudonymization-on-erasure:** an erasure severs a deleted subject's direct identifiers from audit entries while preserving that the action occurred, reconciling immutability with the right to erasure.
  - *Business rules / validation:* The audit-log store is operated in Platform-Admin (references ADMIN-20) and Org-Admin (ORG-22); this story owns the tamper-evidence property, the audit retention bound, the pseudonymization rule, and the access-control model (audit reads are restricted, routed, and themselves audited). Audit PII is in DSAR scope only to the extent it identifies the requesting subject, subject to legitimate-interest retention for security.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A detected integrity-chain break is a Trust & Safety incident and a candidate breach (SEC-17). Audit writes must not be lost under load — if the audit store is unavailable, the audited action is blocked or safely queued, never performed silently un-logged. Backfilling or "correcting" the audit log is prohibited; corrections are new appended entries.

- **[SEC-17] As a Compliance / Data-Protection Officer (COMPLIANCE-DPO), I want a personal-data breach detection, assessment, and notification workflow on a statutory clock, so that an incident is a controlled, defensible response instead of a compounding compliance failure.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* The product has rich *integrity*-incident machinery but no *personal-data*-breach process; any credential-stuffing success or storage leak triggers GDPR Art. 33/34 (72h), US state laws, and FERPA with no workflow and no clock. *(Breach/failure-scenario story.)*
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - Detection signals (anomalous access, cross-tenant read, integrity-chain break, exfil alert) feed a breach-assessment step that classifies severity and scope and starts a **72-hour regulator-notification clock** where applicable.
    - Templated notifications exist for the supervisory authority, affected data subjects, and tenant-scoped ORG-ADMINs; a post-incident register records timeline, scope, root cause, and remediation.
    - A single named owner (COMPLIANCE-DPO, with TRUST-SAFETY-LEAD/PLATFORM-ADMIN as responders) drives the workflow; the audit trail (SEC-16) is the evidence source.
  - *Business rules / validation:* This domain owns the *personal-data* breach process; integrity incidents (sandbox escape, prompt-injection) route through Platform-Admin's incident tooling but escalate here the moment personal data is implicated. Notification content minimizes further exposure (no additional PII in the notice). FERPA-tenant breaches notify the school as controller.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* Uncertain scope defaults to treating data as *potentially* affected for notification-timing purposes rather than delaying past the clock. A breach affecting multiple tenants fans out tenant-scoped notices without leaking one tenant's involvement to another. A near-miss with no confirmed exposure is still logged to the register for pattern analysis. Notification obligations are not waived by remediation speed if exposure occurred.

- **[SEC-18] As the Execution Sandbox (EXEC-SANDBOX), I want a strict untrusted-code security envelope — isolation, no egress, bounded resources — so that running machine-generated code to prove a claim can never become a breach or an escape.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* The sandbox runs arbitrary generated code to mint `Verified·execution`; it is the platform's single largest technical attack surface and an escape is catastrophic for a trust product. *(Failure-scenario-adjacent security story.)*
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - Untrusted code executes in a strongly isolated environment with **default-deny network egress**, no access to secrets/keys/other tenants' data, and enforced CPU/memory/time/output limits.
    - The sandbox is ephemeral (fresh, disposable environment per run) with no persistence of state between runs and no path from executed code to the host, the ledger, or the KMS.
    - Resource-exhaustion, infinite-loop, and fork-bomb style inputs are contained and terminated, surfacing an honest "could not verify by execution" rather than degrading the platform.
  - *Business rules / validation:* The sandbox's *operation* is owned by Platform-Admin (references ADMIN-03); this story owns the security *requirements and threat model* it must satisfy. Executed code cannot make outbound calls that could exfiltrate data or reach a sub-processor; a computational claim that cannot be proven within the envelope reaches at most `Sourced`, never `Verified·execution`, rather than the envelope being relaxed.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* An attempted egress or escape is contained, terminates the run, and alarms to TRUST-SAFETY-LEAD; a confirmed escape is an SEC-17 breach and an SEC-21 containment event. A crafted payload that tries to read another run's memory must be prevented by isolation, not detected after the fact. Sandbox compromise blocks new execution-verification globally (fail-closed) until cleared.

- **[SEC-19] As the Verification Pipeline (VERIFY-PIPELINE) and The Skeptic (SKEPTIC-AI), I want prompt-injection and data-exfiltration defenses across every step that ingests untrusted text, so that a malicious source or answer can't hijack the model into leaking data or forging trust.** — *Priority:* MVP — *Why this priority:* The pipeline and Skeptic process untrusted retrieved sources *and* untrusted learner text, and rendered LLM output is a novel stored-XSS vector; this is the most product-specific security surface. *(Failure-scenario story.)*
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - Untrusted content (retrieved sources, learner free-text) is handled as data, not instructions: system/tool boundaries resist injected directives that would exfiltrate data, escalate a trust state, or bypass the Skeptic.
    - Model outputs and all rendered free-text (lectures, community, discuss, task answers — learner- and LLM-authored) pass strict output encoding, HTML sanitization, a strict Content-Security-Policy, and CSRF protection before reaching the DOM.
    - An injection attempt cannot cause a claim to be certified, a source to be promoted, or one learner's/tenant's data to surface in another's context.
  - *Business rules / validation:* No model output can move a trust state on its own — the epistemic firewall (SEC-02) still requires the authorized capability, so injection at worst produces a *proposed* draft the pipeline/Skeptic must still validate, never an auto-certification. Sub-processor calls carry only the minimum necessary context (data minimization) to limit exfil blast radius. A payload reflected into a lecture is treated as an XSS defect, not "just model behavior."
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A stored payload discovered in rendered content triggers sanitization remediation and, if it enabled session/token theft, SEC-17. An injection that attempts cross-tenant retrieval fails-closed via isolation (SEC-03). Detected injection patterns feed Trust & Safety for source-reputation and abuse signals.

- **[SEC-20] As a Learner (any persona, as a data subject), I want account-takeover attempts contained and my honest signals protected when they occur, so that a credential-stuffing wave or a stolen session can't quietly exfiltrate my learning record.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* Credential-stuffing and session theft are the most common real-world compromise path; honest signals and free-text are sensitive enough that a silent ATO is a reportable breach. *(Breach/failure-scenario story.)*
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - Anomalous-login and credential-stuffing detection (velocity, impossible-travel, breached-password checks) triggers step-up, lockout, or notification, and feeds the breach-assessment step (SEC-17) when success is suspected.
    - A takeover-to-exfil path is guarded: full-portfolio export and bulk data access require step-up re-auth and emit a real-time notification to the account owner (SEC-08).
    - On confirmed compromise, sessions are globally invalidated (references Auth's security cascade), the owner is notified, and affected data scope is assessed for breach notification.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Session/token mechanics and "sign out everywhere" are owned by Auth (AUTH-12, AUTH-11 step-up); this story owns the ATO threat model and the tie-in to breach response and export hardening. Support impersonation is not an ATO but is a *watched* access path: it is scoped, consented, logged, and cannot exfiltrate content beyond the consent scope.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A benign travel/velocity false-positive offers a friction-y but passable step-up, never a permanent lockout. A compromise detected mid-session must not lose the learner's legitimate in-progress data. If exfil via export is confirmed, the exported bundle's existence and scope are part of the breach record.

- **[SEC-21] As a Trust & Safety / Ledger Integrity Lead (TRUST-SAFETY-LEAD), I want a cross-tenant / sandbox-escape containment and forensics runbook, so that a data-isolation failure is contained, evidenced, and remediated instead of spreading silently.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* The two worst-case technical incidents — a cross-tenant leak and a sandbox escape — need a rehearsed containment path distinct from routine ops; without it, the highest-severity events are handled ad hoc. *(Breach/failure-scenario story.)*
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A runbook defines detection → isolate (fail-closed the affected surface, e.g. quarantine the tenant boundary or halt execution-verification) → preserve evidence (immutable audit + snapshots) → assess personal-data scope → remediate → notify.
    - Containment can freeze a suspect account (references AUTH-18) and pause the implicated subsystem globally without deleting evidence; a deletion/erasure in progress is paused, not completed, during an active hold.
    - Post-incident review produces root cause, the fix, and a test that would have caught it (e.g. the continuous isolation test of SEC-03), added to the register (SEC-16/SEC-17).
  - *Business rules / validation:* Containment authority (freeze, quarantine, revoke) never includes epistemic-write — Trust & Safety cannot set a Verified state or resolve a Conflict while responding (firewall holds under incident). Evidence preservation overrides a subject's deletion/export controls for the duration of the hold, time-bounded and reviewed. A personal-data-implicated incident escalates to SEC-17's statutory clock.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A freeze applied in error is fully reversible with no residual read-only state. Containment must not itself cross-contaminate (the response tooling is tenant-aware). If forensics reveals the escape read another tenant's data, both tenants are notified tenant-scoped without disclosing each other's identity.

- **[SEC-22] As an Organization / Teams Admin (ORG-ADMIN), I want evidence of VeriLearn's security posture — SOC 2 / ISO trajectory, penetration testing, and a vulnerability-disclosure program, so that my procurement and security review can approve the purchase.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* Enterprise and school deals stall in security review without third-party assurance; important for revenue but not a runtime control.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - A trust/security page and shareable evidence pack cover the control baselines (encryption SEC-04, isolation SEC-03, access/audit SEC-02/SEC-16, sub-processors SEC-06, breach process SEC-17) mapped to SOC 2 / ISO 27001 criteria and the current certification/attestation status.
    - A recurring third-party penetration-test cadence and remediation-tracking exist; findings above a severity threshold have SLAs.
    - A published vulnerability-disclosure/responsible-reporting channel (and optional bounty) routes external reports into the incident/triage process.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Posture claims must be true and current (honesty invariant applies to security marketing too); a lapsed attestation is disclosed, not implied-current. The evidence pack is provided under the tenant's DPA/NDA where it contains non-public detail.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A pen-test critical finding follows the SEC-17 process if it evidences actual exposure, not just theoretical risk. An external report of a live exploited vulnerability escalates immediately rather than waiting for the normal triage window.

- **[SEC-23] As a Learner (any persona, as a data subject), I want transparency and a human-review path for automated decisions that affect me, so that a rubric grade, readiness prediction, or Teams-visible signal isn't an unaccountable verdict.** — *Priority:* Should-Have — *Why this priority:* Predicted-readiness, calibration, drill-detection, and rubric grading are automated profiling that gate certificates and can influence education/employment outcomes (GDPR Art. 22 territory); fairness here also reinforces the trust-first brand.
  - *Acceptance criteria:*
    - For an automated grade/prediction/adverse signal, the learner can see a plain-language "why this outcome" explanation grounded in the rubric/inputs.
    - A learner can request **human review** of a contested rubric grade or a materially adverse Teams-visible signal; the review is logged and can correct the record where warranted.
    - No solely-automated decision produces an irreversible, materially adverse outcome (e.g. a permanent fail) without an available contest path.
  - *Business rules / validation:* Grading and readiness mechanics are owned by Tasks (24) and Tests (28); this story owns the transparency/contest *right* over them. Human review corrects a graded record within its own domain's rules; it never fabricates a learning signal (the vendor firewall holds) and never rewrites the trust ledger. In FERPA tenants (SEC-13) the right to correct education records subsumes this.
  - *Failure & edge cases:* A contest request during a live test does not pause the test; it queues review of the result. Repeated frivolous contests are rate-limited without denying a genuine one. Explanation copy must not leak other learners' data or the internal model in a way that enables gaming.

### Business rules & invariants

- **SEC-I1 — Data-conduct honesty is binding.** Every public claim about data handling ("we never sell or train on your data," sub-processor register, region, no-retention inference) must be literally true and kept in sync with the actual pipelines and DPAs. A divergence between claim and reality is a compliance incident even absent external exposure. Honesty extends to security marketing and posture status (no implied-current lapsed attestations).
- **SEC-I2 — The security domain specifies controls; it does not re-own surfaces.** MFA/session/SSO mechanics live in Auth; DSAR self-service UI and deletion confirmations in Settings; tenant provisioning, sandbox runtime, and audit store in Platform-Admin; tenant roles in Org-Admin; the cert verify page in Tests. This domain owns the requirement, threat model, invariant, legal basis, and the cross-cutting machinery (breach, sub-processors, retention, residency, tamper-evidence, rights backbone).
- **SEC-I3 — The epistemic firewall holds under every security condition.** No security actor — PLATFORM-ADMIN, TRUST-SAFETY-LEAD, SUPPORT-AGENT, GUARDIAN, ORG-ADMIN, DEVELOPER-API — and no incident-response action, break-glass, or injected prompt can set a trust state, resolve a Conflict, promote a source, or issue a certificate. Security controls protect the ledger; they never write it.
- **SEC-I4 — Fail-closed by default.** Authorization, tenant scoping, region routing, consent enforcement, and audit writes default to *deny/block/queue* on ambiguity, outage, or missing context — never to allow, cross-region, or silently un-logged.
- **SEC-I5 — Free-text is treated as PII from intake.** Learner free-text may contain the author's or a third party's personal data; it is redaction-gated before persistence and before any LLM call, encrypted at rest, minimized in what is sent to sub-processors, and covered by zero-retention/no-train agreements.
- **SEC-I6 — Isolation is a cryptographic and safety property.** Tenant isolation holds at the authorization *and* key layer, is continuously tested, and a confirmed cross-tenant read is a reportable breach for every tenant involved, notified tenant-scoped without cross-disclosure.
- **SEC-I7 — Erasure and certificate/audit retention are reconciled, not in conflict.** Erasure hard-deletes personal data and cascades cleanly, while a minimal non-personal certificate verification record may be retained (Settings I7) and audit entries are pseudonymized (action preserved, subject identifiers severed). Legal holds and active investigations override auto-purge and erasure until lifted.
- **SEC-I8 — Minors are protected by default, not by opt-in.** Under-threshold accounts are hard-blocked from personal-data processing until verifiable consent (guardian or FERPA school authorization), and inherit minor-safe defaults (no public community profile, no public cert name, suppressed marketing, data minimization). Age corrections tighten, never loosen.
- **SEC-I9 — Every privileged, epistemic-write, and data-rights action is auditable and tamper-evident.** The audit log is append-only and integrity-chained; actions that cannot be logged do not proceed silently; corrections are new appends, never edits.
- **SEC-I10 — Personal-data incidents run on a statutory clock.** Any confirmed or suspected exposure of personal data enters the breach workflow with a named owner and a 72-hour regulator-notification assessment; uncertainty defaults to treating data as potentially affected for timing.

### Cross-domain dependencies

- **Auth (20):** Owns MFA/passkey/session/SSO/step-up mechanics (AUTH-11/12/13), tenant isolation baseline (AUTH-20), freeze (AUTH-18), deletion confirmation (AUTH-19), and the security cascade. This domain adds phishing-resistant/dual-control policy for epistemic-write roles, the crypto-layer isolation requirement, ATO detection, and breach tie-in — it does not re-implement the auth substrate.
- **Settings / Privacy (33):** Owns the self-service export UI/format (SETTINGS-13), DPO DSAR fulfillment surface (SETTINGS-14), minor handling surface (SETTINGS-15), deletion/danger-zone (SETTINGS-19), and privacy toggles (SETTINGS-12), plus invariants I4/I5/I7. This domain owns the rights-request backbone (identity verification, SLA, orchestration), erasure cascade contract, consent/lawful-basis register, and export exfil-hardening behind those surfaces.
- **Platform-Admin (36):** Operates tenant provisioning + isolation (ADMIN-01), the execution sandbox runtime (ADMIN-03), the audit store (ADMIN-20), break-glass, and integrity-incident tooling. This domain specifies the sandbox security envelope, audit tamper-evidence and pseudonymization, and escalation from integrity incidents to personal-data breach response.
- **Org-Admin (35):** Owns tenant roles/RBAC definitions, offboarding data disposition (ORG-14), the visibility policy and its DPO constraint (ORG-11/ORG-21), and the audit surface (ORG-22). This domain adds least-privilege/separation-of-duties invariants, the compliance-agreements surface, FERPA designation, and tenant-scoped breach notification.
- **Tasks (24) & Tests/Certificates (28):** Own rubric grading, readiness prediction, and the certificate verify page (TEST-11/12). This domain adds automated-decision transparency/contest rights, minor no-public-name defaults on the verify page, and enumeration/harvesting resistance requirements.
- **Pipeline / Skeptic / Sandbox (22/25):** Consume untrusted sources and learner text and route to LLM sub-processors. This domain specifies prompt-injection/exfil defenses, sub-processor DPAs/zero-retention, PII redaction before LLM calls, and data-residency-aware inference routing.
- **Community (29):** Deletion anonymizes contributions and promotion makes content durable ledger canon. This domain requires PII-away canonicalization on promotion so erasure succeeds without holing the ledger, and output sanitization on rendered posts.
- **Integrations / API (38):** SSO/LTI/webhooks and export consumers are bound by scoped tokens, rate limits, region/consent enforcement, and the rule that integrations never mutate the ledger or bypass these controls.
- **Billing (34):** Owns tokenized payment data (no raw card storage); this domain covers it under the encryption baseline and retention schedule.

### Key technical requirements

- **Identity & access:** Phishing-resistant MFA (WebAuthn/passkey/hardware) mandated for epistemic-write and vendor roles; dual-control (2-of-N) + step-up on certificate-eligible certifications; least-privilege, fail-closed, separation-of-duties RBAC with time-boxed, reason-logged, non-epistemic break-glass.
- **Isolation & encryption:** Hard multi-tenant isolation with default-deny tenant scoping, per-tenant envelope encryption keys, and a continuous automated isolation test; TLS 1.2+ everywhere, AES-256 at rest for all learner data classes and backups, managed KMS with documented rotation and key/data-plane separation.
- **Free-text & sub-processor governance:** PII-detection/redaction gate before persistence and before any LLM call; maintained sub-processor register (public + in-app); zero-retention/no-train DPAs with every inference provider; data minimization of context sent to sub-processors; per-tenant data-region pinning over storage *and* inference with SCCs/TIA for unavoidable transfers.
- **Data-subject rights backbone:** Verified-identity intake for access/portability/rectification/erasure; faithful ledger-inclusive export; SLA tracking; cross-store erasure cascade with atomic rollback, backup rolling-purge, cert decoupling, community canonicalization, and legal-hold override; step-up + notification + own-data scoping on full exports.
- **Consent & minors:** Lawful-basis processing register; cookie/ePrivacy consent-management layer with per-purpose, versioned, source-enforced choices; hard COPPA age-gate; verifiable guardian consent with guardian↔dependent linkage; FERPA education-record treatment and school-official agreements; minor-safe defaults enforced by policy.
- **Audit & tamper-evidence:** Append-only, hash-chained audit log capturing actor/scope/basis/before-after for privileged, epistemic-write, and rights actions; integrity-checked trust-state transitions; pseudonymization-on-erasure; bounded retention; restricted, routed, self-auditing log reads; block/queue-on-unavailable (never silently un-logged).
- **Untrusted-code & untrusted-prompt defense:** Ephemeral, network-egress-denied, resource-bounded, secret-isolated execution sandbox that fails closed on escape/exhaustion; prompt-injection/exfil defenses treating retrieved and learner content as data; strict CSP, context-aware output encoding, HTML sanitization, CSRF protection, and secure/httpOnly/SameSite cookies on every surface rendering free-text.
- **Incident & posture:** Personal-data breach workflow (detect→assess→72h notify→register) with a named owner and templated regulator/subject/tenant notices; cross-tenant/sandbox-escape containment-and-forensics runbook with evidence preservation overriding deletion during holds; SOC 2 / ISO 27001 evidence, recurring third-party pen-testing with remediation SLAs, and a vulnerability-disclosure program.
- **Automated-decision fairness:** Learner-facing "why this outcome" explanations and a logged human-review/contest path for rubric grades, readiness predictions, and materially adverse Teams-visible signals, without fabricating learning signals or rewriting the ledger.
