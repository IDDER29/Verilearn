Area 2 · Learn — topic workspace

One opened topic, four tabs

The heart of the product. After a topic is verified it opens into a workspace with a shared top bar and a four-tab strip. The learner reads, applies, resolves disputes, and audits the evidence — all against the same claim ledger.

T1LectureRead it. Claims are underlined by trust level.
T2TasksApply it. Write-in answers graded on a rubric.
T3ConflictsResolve disputes the Skeptic raised.
T4SourcesAudit which source backs each claim.

Freely switchable: these are tabs, not a wizard. The reading order above is the intended path, but the trust ledger is shared — a claim underlined in the Lecture is the same object adjudicated in Conflicts and traced in Sources.

▾ Follow-up · open micro-chapter

Recall isn't understanding. Tasks force the learner to produce the idea in their own words, then grades it against evidence.

1
Task list + statusEach task is to do / revise / passed. Explain and Reason types.
2
Prompt + write-in3–5 sentences in the learner's own words. Submit is gated on a minimum length.
3
Rubric gradingScored against a source-derived rubric; each criterion shows hit or missing with a % score. ≥75% passes.
4
Follow-up → micro-chapter → model answerA partial pass gets a targeted follow-up question and an expandable, source-grounded micro-chapter. A full pass reveals the model answer + Next task.

Disagreement is a first-class object. When the Skeptic or the execution sandbox flags an overreach, the learner adjudicates it rather than the app hiding it.

1
Conflict listOpen vs resolved, tagged by who raised it (Skeptic / Execution). Resolved items dim out.

The disputed claimThe exact claim in question plus why it's contested (e.g. "fails with negative weights").
3
Competing positionsTwo mapped stances with their support. Picking one seeds the resolution text.
4
