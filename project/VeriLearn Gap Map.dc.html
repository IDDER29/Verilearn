Every known gap, in one place

A proposed home for the concept the prototype already leans on but never gives a screen. Misconceptions and weak spots get named, tracked through a lifecycle, and resurfaced until they're genuinely closed — instead of vanishing into a review queue.

Why this page exists

The gap map is referenced all over the app — ＋ Add to gap map in review, "named gaps, statuses, discussion threads" in Privacy, "Reset gap map" in Settings — but there is nowhere to actually see your gaps. This page closes that loop and turns Progress's "Where to focus" into something actionable.

InA gap is caughtFrom a wrong-idea, missed drill, partial task, or dispute.
MapIt's trackedNamed, given a status & severity, linked to its source.
WorkYou close itRevisit the section, drill it, discuss it.
OutIt resurfacesReopens automatically if you lapse again.

Not another queue: the review deck tests recall on a schedule; the gap map tracks specific known weaknesses as durable objects — with a status you can see and a thread you can revisit.

Where gaps come from

Four sources already in the app

Review · wrong idea
Acknowledging a misconception on a card — the existing "＋ Add to gap map" button.
Missed seeded drill
Failing to catch a deliberately-wrong claim — a blind spot worth tracking.
Partial task
A rubric criterion the learner keeps missing across attempts.
Resolved dispute
A conflict whose resolution revealed a shaky underlying assumption.
Lifecycle

How a gap moves

OpenNewly caught and actively hurting. Shows on the board and in "Where to focus".
WatchingImproving — recalled correctly recently, but not yet trusted. On probation.
ClosedConsistently solid across reviews and drills. Archived, still searchable.
ReopenedA later lapse auto-reopens a closed gap — the honesty mechanism.
